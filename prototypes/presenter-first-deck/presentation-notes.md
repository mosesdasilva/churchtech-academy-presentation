# ChurchTech Academy Presenter-First Notes

This document holds the fuller talking points for the presenter-first prototype. The deck is intentionally lighter, so the speaker can explain details from here instead of placing all content directly on slides.

## Slide 1 - Opening

- Explain that this prototype assumes the teacher allows a separate written document.
- Emphasize that the slide deck is now built to support speaking, pacing, and memory instead of carrying every paragraph.
- State that the ministry vision itself has not changed.

## Slide 2 - Problem

- Small churches often rely on volunteers with little formal training.
- Medium churches usually have some equipment and structure but still struggle with consistency.
- Large churches still need repeatable onboarding, standards, and volunteer multiplication.

## Slide 3 - Opportunity

- Highlight the scale: Dallas County, Texas, the United States, and Latin America.
- Explain that the team already carries ministry relationships, technical understanding, and bilingual or bicultural reach.

## Slide 4 - Vision

- Vision: equip the global church for excellence in production and worship.
- Mission: empower churches with accessible, free, and spiritually aligned production training.
- Use the Train / Strengthen / Reach structure as the verbal flow.

## Slide 5 - Spiritual Foundation

- Prayer is foundational to the ministry's direction.
- Biblical principles include stewardship, order that serves edification, and equipping the saints.
- Mention that the full prayer statement and Scripture passages remain in the source proposal.

## Slide 6 - Goals

- Accessibility: remove barriers to entry.
- Excellence: strengthen clarity and readiness.
- Empowerment: train volunteers and leaders to grow.
- Impact: improve Gospel communication through technology.
- Prayer: keep the ministry spiritually aligned.

## Slide 7 - Operating Model

- Explain that the academy trains teams, standardizes ministry systems, and strengthens worship environments.
- Tie this back to practical church outcomes rather than abstract education language.

## Slide 8 - Curriculum

- Core areas: audio, video and broadcast, lighting, worship ministry, leadership and team management.
- Mention that the curriculum is intended to be original, ministry-specific, and practically applied.

## Slide 9 - Pathway

- Levels: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, Team Leader.
- Support programs: live discussion, Q&A, and future multilingual access.
- Explain that the pathway helps churches know where to start and how to grow.

## Slide 10 - Delivery

- Teaching methods include demonstration, video-based instruction, and discussion.
- Materials include production equipment, slide support, handouts, hosting, and multilingual assets.
- Emphasize that this can begin with a modest but real ministry production setup.

## Slide 11 - Organization

- Leadership structure: Founding Director over Operations Administrator.
- Operations coordinates Production Team, Partnerships and Marketing, and Volunteer Support.
- The point of the slide is structural clarity, not full job-description detail.

## Slide 12 - Review

- Evaluation starts after the pilot phase.
- Weekly, monthly, quarterly, and annual reviews each answer a different level of question.
- The main review lenses are spiritual health, practical usefulness, and strategic improvement.

## Slide 13 - Revision

- Evaluation should result in action.
- Example revisions include shortening long course tracks or accelerating multilingual expansion.
- Present the ministry as adaptable and willing to improve.

## Slide 14 - Closing

- Restate that ChurchTech Academy strengthens the people behind the service.
- Emphasize that the presenter-first approach improves live delivery while the documentation preserves the full written argument.
