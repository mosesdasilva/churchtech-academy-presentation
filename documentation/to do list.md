## **Step 5 - Programs and Curricula**

**Assigned to:** Moses

### **What this step needs to produce**

Write the full Programs and Curricula section for ChurchTech Academy. This section should explain how the ministry will structure its training programs, what kinds of training tracks will be offered, how those programs will serve churches of different sizes, and how the curriculum will be organized.

### **What to include**

- Explain that ChurchTech Academy will build structured learning pathways specifically for church production teams.
- State whether the curriculum will be original, existing, or a combination of both.
- Explain that the curriculum should stay biblically aligned, practical, scalable, and ministry focused.
- List the main program areas:
  - Audio training
  - Video and broadcast training
  - Lighting training
  - Stage and service support
  - Worship and production leadership
- For audio training, describe content such as signal flow, microphone technique, stage patching, monitor mixing, front-of-house mixing, troubleshooting, and volunteer communication.
- For video and broadcast training, describe content such as camera operation, switching, framing, graphics workflow, livestream execution, and service communication.
- For lighting training, describe content such as lighting foundations, programming basics, worship lighting philosophy, and visual consistency.
- For stage and service support, describe content such as stage setup, transitions, team coordination, backstage readiness, and service reliability.
- For worship and production leadership, describe content such as team health, spiritual posture, service planning, volunteer development, and leadership communication.
- Explain how the curriculum should reflect the needs of:
  - Small churches
  - Medium churches
  - Large churches
- Show that small churches need simple, repeatable, step-by-step, low-cost training.
- Show that medium churches need systems that improve consistency across multiple volunteers.
- Show that large churches need standardized training libraries, role-based tracks, and quality benchmarks for onboarding.
- Explain that the curriculum should include beginner, intermediate, and advanced pathways.
- State that courses may be packaged as on-demand video lessons with downloadable notes, checklists, discussion prompts, and practical ministry applications.
- Mention that additional ministry-support programs may include live workshops, instructor Q&A sessions, volunteer bootcamps, and church team intensives.

### **Final writing goal**

By the end of Step 5, Moses should produce a complete section that explains exactly what ChurchTech Academy will teach, how the curriculum will be structured, and how it will serve different church contexts.

## **Step 6 - Method and Material**

**Assigned to:** Sidney

### **What this step needs to produce**

Write the full Method and Material section for ChurchTech Academy. This section should explain how the ministry will teach the content and what tools, equipment, materials, and spaces will be needed to deliver the training effectively.

### **What to include**

- State that ChurchTech Academy will use multiple teaching methods to serve different learning styles and make the training practical for real ministry teams.
- Include at least three teaching methods, but preferably cover all of these:
  - Lecture and demonstration
  - Discussion and Q&A
  - Hands-on practicum or on-the-job training
  - Video-based instruction
  - Guided self-study
- For lecture and demonstration, explain that instructors will teach core concepts while visually showing equipment, software, or workflows in action.
- For discussion and Q&A, explain that participants will process ideas, solve ministry-specific problems, and apply principles in context.
- For hands-on practicum or on-the-job training, explain that learners will apply lessons during real rehearsals, services, and livestreams.
- For video-based instruction, explain that learners will use short, well-structured visual lessons that can be reviewed repeatedly.
- For guided self-study, explain that downloadable notes, checklists, and action steps will help volunteers continue learning outside the lesson environment.
- List the materials, facilities, and equipment needed.
- Include production equipment such as cameras, tripods, switcher access, microphones, audio interface or console access, lighting examples, and related church production gear.
- Include teaching equipment such as a laptop, presentation monitor, projector or TV screen, internet access, microphones, and presentation slides.
- Include course materials such as lesson outlines, printable handouts, note sheets, checklists, training pathways, and certificates of completion.
- Include facilities such as recording space, editing workspace, and access to real church production environments for demonstrations and case studies.
- Include platform materials such as hosting infrastructure, course pages, user logins, multilingual subtitle or dubbing assets, and communication systems for subscribers.

### **Final writing goal**

By the end of Step 6, Sidney should produce a clear section that explains both how the ministry teaches and what resources are required to make that teaching possible.

## **Step 8 - Evaluation**

**Assigned to:** Guilherme

### **What this step needs to produce**

Write the full Evaluation section for ChurchTech Academy. This section should explain when the ministry will be evaluated, how often evaluation will happen, what tools will be used, who will participate, who will receive results, and what questions will guide the evaluation process.

### **What to include**

- State that ChurchTech Academy will be evaluated regularly to determine whether the platform is achieving its spiritual, educational, and organizational goals.
- Explain when the first evaluation will begin.
- Use the idea that the first evaluation should happen after the pilot phase and first course releases, once enough participants have completed lessons to provide useful feedback.
- Explain how often evaluation will happen:
  - Weekly
  - Monthly
  - Quarterly
  - Annually
- For weekly evaluation, describe informal internal review of production quality, content progress, and workflow bottlenecks.
- For monthly evaluation, describe review of subscriber engagement, lesson completion, church feedback, and platform performance.
- For quarterly evaluation, describe formal review of course effectiveness, strategic goals, and ministry impact.
- For annual evaluation, describe high-level review of mission alignment, financial sustainability, curriculum growth, and expansion priorities.
- List the evaluation tools:
  - Participant feedback forms
  - Course completion data
  - Engagement metrics such as watch time and retention
  - Church partner interviews
  - Team review meetings
  - Financial and subscription reports
- Explain who will be involved in the evaluation process:
  - Executive leadership
  - Operations leadership
  - Curriculum and content leaders
  - Selected instructors
  - Participating churches or users
- Explain who will receive the results.
- State that leadership should review the results internally and share summary findings with key team members, instructors, and strategic ministry partners when appropriate.
- Include evaluation questions such as:
  - Are the lessons biblically aligned and spiritually healthy?
  - Are the courses practical and clear for volunteers and church staff?
  - Are subscribers completing the training and applying it in ministry settings?
  - Which programs create the greatest impact for churches of different sizes?
  - What technical, instructional, or organizational improvements are needed next?

### **Final writing goal**

By the end of Step 8, Guilherme should produce a structured evaluation section that clearly shows how the ministry will measure effectiveness and make wise decisions going forward.

By the end of Step 9, Davi should produce a section that shows how ChurchTech Academy will adapt after evaluation and continue improving its ministry impact.
