# Step 5: Programs and Curricula

## Question 1

ChurchTech Academy will create its own curriculum because the ministry is training people for real church production environments, not controlled classroom settings. The goal is to prepare volunteers, leaders, and ministry teams for the day-to-day realities of serving during live worship services, where reliability, clarity, and practical readiness matter. Writing our own curriculum allows us to build training directly around real church situations, common service challenges, and ministry-based workflows that churches can actually apply.

The curriculum will be distinct because it will focus on the real life of church production rather than only teaching theory. Instead of stopping at concepts, ChurchTech Academy will show how those concepts work in practice during actual ministry situations. Lessons will be built around realistic church examples so learners can see what good service preparation, troubleshooting, communication, and execution look like in context.

The main teaching principle behind the curriculum will be learning by doing. ChurchTech Academy will emphasize practical application, step-by-step demonstration, and hands-on ministry problem solving so that learners do not only understand production ideas, but also know how to carry them out in service. The purpose is to help students grow through active practice, because production ministry is learned most effectively through doing, repetition, and real-world application.

The curriculum will be packaged primarily as a video-based course system. Students will move through training in a clear sequence, with weekly sections and short lessons that focus on one principle, skill, or responsibility at a time. This structure will allow the curriculum to remain organized, easy to follow, and practical for churches that need simple, progressive training pathways.

## Question 2

ChurchTech Academy will not rely on an existing curriculum as its main teaching foundation. Instead, the ministry will use its own curriculum, developed from the direct experience of the team members who are already actively serving in ministry. This approach reflects the belief that a stronger and more relevant training program can be created from real church production experience than from borrowed material designed for other settings.

The team already carries years of hands-on experience in church production, worship environments, and ministry service. That experience provides a strong base for building curriculum that is practical, tested, and closely aligned with the actual needs of local churches. Because the training is being built by people who understand the realities of church ministry from inside the work itself, ChurchTech Academy believes it can create a more effective curriculum than simply adopting an outside program.

## Question 3

The curriculum will be structured around four levels of experience and responsibility rather than around age alone. This approach fits church production ministry more effectively because students may begin serving at different ages while still needing the same kind of progression in skill, consistency, and responsibility. The goal is to create a clear pathway that helps each learner grow from entry-level service into dependable ministry leadership.

Within each core department, such as audio, video, lighting, or stage support, the curriculum will be organized into four levels: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, and Leadership. The Beginner level will be for first-time volunteers and those who are new to production ministry. This level may be especially helpful for teenagers, students, or new volunteers serving in kids ministry, youth ministry, or other simpler service environments where they can begin learning basic concepts and ministry habits. The Intermediate level will be for developing volunteers who are becoming more consistent and more serious in their training. This level may fit those serving in youth ministry, midweek gatherings, or supporting roles where they are building stronger technical understanding and reliability.

The Advanced level will be for volunteers who are ready to serve in the main service environment and carry greater responsibility during regular church gatherings. At this stage, students should be growing in consistency, troubleshooting ability, and confidence in real ministry situations. The Leadership level will be for those who oversee teams, guide departments, train other volunteers, and carry responsibility for the health and direction of a ministry area.

This structure allows ChurchTech Academy to support different kinds of learners while keeping the curriculum organized around ministry readiness and responsibility. It creates a pathway where a student can begin with simple service opportunities, grow into advanced weekly ministry roles, and eventually be equipped to lead others.

## Question 4

In addition to the main curriculum, ChurchTech Academy will build supporting programs that help students and churches follow a clearer growth path through ministry training. The main curriculum itself will remain the video-based course system described earlier. These additional programs will serve as guidance structures around the course content, helping learners understand where to begin, what to study next, and how to grow from a first-time volunteer into a dependable team member or ministry leader.

One of these programs will be a Volunteer Onboarding Program. This program will serve as a clear entry track for first-time team members who are new to church production. It will help new volunteers understand the basics of serving, the expectations of ministry teams, and the first concepts they need to know before taking on greater responsibility.

Another program will be Role-Based Ministry Pathways. These pathways will guide learners through specific ministry areas such as audio, video, lighting, stage support, and leadership. Each pathway will function like a roadmap, showing the key concepts, skills, and responsibilities that a student needs to understand in order to improve in that area. This structure will help learners see a clear progression instead of feeling lost or unsure about what to learn next.

ChurchTech Academy also plans to build a Leadership Development Program for those who will oversee teams, train volunteers, and multiply ministry capacity in their churches. This program will focus on preparing department leaders who can guide others, maintain healthy team culture, and strengthen the consistency and effectiveness of church production ministries.

As part of these pathway programs, ChurchTech Academy may also create roadmap-style visual guides that show the order of key concepts a learner should study within each ministry area. These guides could use a diagram or box-based layout that helps students understand the full path of growth in a role. In some cases, the roadmap may also include hand-picked supplemental recommendations, such as articles, videos, or learning links, for students who want to study further beyond the main course material. These outside resources would not replace the ministry's own curriculum and would not be required as core material. They would only serve as carefully selected optional guidance for students who want to grow further on their own.
