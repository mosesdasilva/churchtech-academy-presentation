# **Business Proposal: ChurchTech Academy**

**Equipping the Church for Excellence in Production and Worship**

---

## **Executive Summary**

ChurchTech Academy is a subscription-based online platform designed to train churches in every aspect of production and worship. Modeled after “Netflix,” the platform will deliver lessons in audio, video, lighting, stage design, broadcast, and worship leadership.

The heart of the business is to make **high-quality production training accessible and affordable** for churches of all sizes. Many congregations struggle to train volunteers effectively, relying on inconsistent mentorship or expensive conferences. By gathering respected instructors from influential Dallas-area churches with different backgrounds and cultures, and dubbing lessons in Portuguese and Spanish, ChurchTech Academy will address both the U.S. and Latin American markets.

Our mission is simple: **equip the global church for excellence, so worship can reach further, louder, and clearer for the glory of God.**

## **Business Description**

### **Mission Statement**

To empower churches worldwide with accessible, affordable, and spiritually aligned production training.

### **Vision Statement**

Equipping the global church for excellence in production and worship, creating lasting impact in both local congregations and the broader Kingdom.

### **Values**

* **Accessibility** \- Removing barriers to training by offering a low-cost, on-demand resource.  
* **Excellence** \- Teaching both technical skills with depth in church production.  
* **Empowerment** \- Equipping volunteers and leaders to serve their churches with confidence.  
* **Kingdom Impact** \- Using media and technology to expand the reach of the Gospel.

### **SMART Goals**

* **Specific**: Provide at least 20 high-quality courses by the end of year one.  
* **Measurable**: Reach 500 individual subscribers and 50 churches within the first 12 months.  
* **Achievable**: Leverage Dallas’ vast church network and CFNI relationships for instructors.  
* **Relevant**: Churches increasingly depend on production for in-person and online ministry.  
* **Time-Bound**: Platform launch in January 2026; multilingual expansion by year two.

## **Products and Services**

* **On-Demand Video Courses**: Topics include audio mixing, lighting design, video directing, broadcast mixing, worship leadership, and stage design.  
* **Regionalized Access**: All lessons are dubbed into Portuguese and Spanish for Latin American audiences.  
* **Subscription Plans**:  
  * Individuals: $19.99/month.  
  * Churches: $99-199/month for multi-user access.  
* **Certificates of Completion**: Recognition for volunteers who complete training tracks.  
* **Premium Add-Ons**: Live masterclasses, instructor Q\&A, and one-on-one consulting for churches.

## **Market Analysis**

### **Target Market \- Churches by Size**

Unlike businesses defined by demographics, our customers are churches, which can be segmented by size:

1. **Small Churches (under 200 members)**  
   * Typically run by volunteers with little to no formal training.  
   * Limited budget for conferences or external training.  
   * Benefit most from affordable, step-by-step lessons.  
2. **Medium Churches (200-1,000 members)**  
   * Often have basic production infrastructure but lack depth of knowledge.  
   * Desire to elevate quality for livestreams and worship services.  
   * Seek tools to train multiple volunteers efficiently.  
3. **Large Churches (1,000+ members)**  
   * Already have staff but constantly onboard volunteers.  
   * Need standardized training to ensure consistency across teams.  
   * Early adopters and key influencers for broader credibility.

### **Market Size**

* **Dallas County**: 1,500+ churches.  
* **Texas**: 22,000-30,000 churches.  
* **United States**: \~357,000 congregations.  
* **Latin America**: Tens of thousands of churches, with Brazil and Mexico representing massive potential.

### **Industry Analysis**

The online education sector is booming. While secular platforms (Mix with The Masters, PureMix) exist, the faith-based church production space doesn’t have a strong competition. Our project differentiates by leveraging Dallas’ big and diverse church ecosystem and offering multilingual access.

## **Marketing Strategy**

* **Digital Marketing**: Social media campaigns using short clips on Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube.  
* **YouTube Channel and Monetized Content**: Alongside short-form content for Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts, we will run a dedicated YouTube channel releasing 10-15 minute educational videos on church production. These videos will draw from course material or simplified lessons tailored for a wider audience. This approach builds an engaged learning community, provides ongoing free value that attracts potential subscribers, and doubles as a revenue stream through YouTube monetization (ads and partnerships). Over time, the channel becomes both a marketing funnel and an independent income source, reinforcing brand trust and visibility.  
* **Partnerships**: With Dallas churches for pilot testing.  
* **Conferences**: Presence at worship and production events.  
* **Referral Discounts**: Incentivize churches to share the platform with others.

## **Funding Logistics**

### **Initial Investment**

The business will require an initial $10,000 investment to cover video production gear, initial platform setup, and launch marketing. This ensures professional-quality lessons and a strong foundation for the subscription model.

### **Instructor Royalties**

To attract and retain top talent, ChurchTech Academy will pay 10% of total revenue to instructors as royalties. Payments are distributed proportionally based on viewership:

* If an instructor’s lessons account for 20% of total platform views in a month, they will receive 20% of the instructor revenue pool (i.e., 2% of total monthly revenue).  
* This ensures fair compensation, incentivizes instructors to create engaging content, and motivates them to promote their lessons to their own networks.

### **Expense Breakdown (2026–2028)**

Our main costs will scale with the growth of the platform and subscriber base, and these are the categories

* Platform Development & Hosting – $15,000 in 2026, growing to $40,000 by 2028\.  
* Marketing & Sales – $15,000 in 2026, increasing to $70,000 by 2028, to expand reach and enter Latin America.  
* Administrative & Legal – $5,000 in 2026, growing to $15,000 by 2028, covering contracts, compliance, and accounting.  
* Instructor Payments (10% of revenue) – $7,174 in 2026, scaling to $28,697 by 2028\.

### **Revenue Projections**

* 2026: $71,742 (split evenly between individuals and churches).  
* 2027: $143,484 (steady subscriber growth).  
* 2028: $286,968 (platform scales into global markets, including Portuguese and Spanish dubbing).
