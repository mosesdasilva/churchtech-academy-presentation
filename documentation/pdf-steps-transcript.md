Every organization has a set of core values that describe its identity and
culture. Together with the mission and the vision of the organization (which

DNA. While other things in the organization may change with the seasons, the

Cross Chapel discipleship ministry will offer three different levels of Bible study programs for
Christians: new, young, and mature believers. These programs will be available on Sunday mornings
(9am-10am), Wednesdays (7pm-8pm), and on select weekends. All three Bible study programs will

Method: Example
Cross Chapel ministry programs will incorporate a diversity of teaching methods that will appeal to
all learning styles. Each program will feature a specific style of learning that will help engage and
transform the learner. These will include:
Lectures: This method will appeal to auditory and visual learners. An oral presentation delivered
by a knowledgeable person will be paired with PowerPoint slides. (Bible study, worship course,
discipleship, and evangelism programs)
Discussion: This method will appeal to auditory, visual, and kinesthetic learners. Members will
participate in group discussion through the sharing of opinions, ideas, and information about a subject.
(Bible study, worship course, discipleship, and evangelism programs)

### Page 19

Method: Example
Question and Answer: This method will appeal to auditory, visual, and collaborative learners.
The teacher will ask a series of questions to help participants explore the subject. (Bible study,
worship course, discipleship, and evangelism programs)
Participation: This method will appeal to kinesthetic, auditory, and dynamic learners who benefit
from physical participation. They will give testimonies, listen to and react to stories, and participate in
question and answering. (Bible study, worship course, discipleship, and evangelism programs)
Workshops: This method will appeal to kinesthetic, auditory, and visual learners. Participants will
sharpen their ministry tools through hands-on training. (Evangelism program)

### Page 20

Method: Example
Inductive Study: This method will appeal to auditory and visual learners. Participants will approach
God’s Word using three basic steps that move from a general overview to specifics. The steps are observation
(what does it say?), interpretation (what does it mean?), and application (what does it mean for my life?).
(Bible study program)
Video Presentation: This method will appeal to visual and auditory learners. They will benefit from short
videos that give creative teaching principles.  (Evangelism program)
Writing in Workbooks: This method will appeal to visual and kinesthetic learners. They will answer
questions and take notes in their workbooks. (Discipleship, and evangelism programs)
Self-Study: This method will appeal to visual, kinesthetic, and analytic learners. They will study outside
of class to go deeper in the material for personal application. (Bible study, discipleship, and evangelism
programs)

Step 7 Description of Key Positions Example
Executive leadership: This team includes the senior pastor and elders. Their primary roles are to provide vision,
leadership, and guidance for the congregation. Also this team appoints, hires, and makes key decisions for the church.
Senior leadership: This team includes pastoral staff that oversees the children, youth, young adult, worship, and other
ministries. These leaders meet and report regularly to the executive leadership team to set goals and objectives, to
make revisions as needed.
Discipleship leaders: This team includes staff members that serve and oversee the Bible study curricula, outreach
programs, prayer meetings, and discipleship programs. These leaders meet and report regularly to the executive
leadership team to set goals and objectives, plan classes, choose discipleship curricula, suggest facilitators, evaluate
the ministry, and make revision as needed.
Ministry volunteers/Hospitality team: These teams include volunteers who serve in or assist with the various church
programs, events, services, and outreach programs. Their roles include serving food, helping with childcare, setting up
chairs and tables, cleaning up, ushering and greeting guest, singing and playing instruments, etc. The ministry team
reports to the senior leadership and the hospitality team reports to the discipleship leaders.

Evaluation Method Example
The various ministry programs and curricula will be evaluated weekly, monthly, quarterly, and annually
using the process, impact, and summative evaluation methods. These will measure how well a
program or curriculum is achieving its objectives and is staying aligned with the biblical principles and
the core values of the ministry. An informal evaluation will be given by the discipleship leaders
monitoring the weekly classes. They will report how well the class and curriculum was received by
participants to the executive leadership. Also, a formal evaluation will be conducted at the end each
course by the executive leadership. The participants will fill out class evaluation forms scoring the
class content and instruction.

### Page 11

Evaluation Method Example
Process Method: This method will be conducted weekly by the discipleship team that oversees the various discipleship
ministries. This method will best serve to measure the quality of the program, and to examine the strategies and procedures
involved in the implementation of our programs. The discipleship team will report their weekly evaluation to the executive
leadership on how well the discipleship process is moving the participants toward spiritual maturity in their faith.
Impact Method: This method will be conducted monthly by the executive leadership and discipleship leaders by focusing on
long-term results of the program, and changes or improvements to the overall program. This method is used to measure how
effectively the objectives of the program have been achieved and the impact of the program immediately after its completion. The
final results of the impact evaluation method will be discussed among the executive leadership. The results should identify
changes in behavior that exhibit Christ-like character in the participants.
Summative Method: This method will be conducted quarterly using class evaluation forms by the discipleship leadership. It will
be given after the program is completed to determine whether to continue with the program or to end it. This evaluation method
will also help us determine whether a program is sustainable in its effectiveness. The executive leadership will review the class
evaluation forms and give the class evaluation summary to the discipleship leaders.

### Page 14

Revision Example
Based on the informal and formal evaluations conducted, the executive leadership may decide to make some revisions
for improving the overall programs and curricula offered.
The following are hypothetical revisions:
Bible Study Program: After several months of offering three different levels of Bible study programs and curricula, the
executive leadership has determined that one joint Bible study once a month for all participants is needed. The class
evaluation forms revealed that the class content and instruction scored excellently. However, many participants
commented that they wanted to have a joint Bible study for all to attend once a month. Through prayer and after
several meetings, the executive leadership decided to use a devotional method for the joint Bible study instead of an
inductive Bible study. The devotional method will allow participants to take one passage of Scripture and prayerfully
meditate on it for practical application.

### Page 15

Revision Example
Worship Course: The “night of worship” once a month has been growing in attendance with
more families bringing their kids. This has created a problem for the childcare ministry of having
too many kids and not enough volunteers to serve. As a result, the executive worship pastor
has recruited more volunteers within the church to help serve in childcare.
Discipleship Training: The discipleship training program has inspired many of the church
members to start serving and volunteering in various ministries within the church. However,
many of the church members are serving in one or more ministries that occasionally overlap.
This has created scheduling problems for ministry departments and volunteers. After meeting
for discussion, the discipleship leaders created a Google Doc sheet for all ministry departments
to schedule volunteers accordingly.

### Page 16

Revision Example
Evangelism Workshop: After several months of sending outreach teams into local communities and
inner cities, many of the participants wished they could reach their own unsaved family members and
close friends with the gospel. The executive leadership and evangelism leaders met and decided that
one of the four Saturday mornings would be dedicated to hosting a “family & friends” outreach. This
new outreach will provide opportunities for church participants to invite their family and friends to
church, where they can be reached with the gospel and love of Christ.
